Flowerpedia

Annual Flowers

Annual flowers complete their life cycle in one growing season. They germinate, bloom, set seed, and die within a single year.

  • Marigolds

  • Petunias

  • Zinnias

  • Sunflowers

Biennial Flowers

Biennial flowers take two years to complete their life cycle. In the first year, they grow leaves, stems, and roots, and in the second year, they bloom, set seed, and die.

  • Foxgloves

  • Hollyhocks

  • Canterbury Bells

  • Parsley

Perennial Flowers

Perennial flowers live for more than two years. They typically bloom each year during their growing season and go dormant during the off-season.

  • Coneflowers

  • Peonies

  • Daylilies

  • Lavender
